Question 2 (***)#

Flow occurs over a spillway with a width of 100 m. The spillway has a cross section as shown below. The water depth on the upstream side of the spillway (y1) is 20 m, and on the downstream side (y2) is 1.5 m. The friction energy headloss over the spillway is 1.2 m. Determine the horizontal force of the water on the spillway.

Answer

\( 147.7 \; MN\; to \; the \; right \)

Hint

Apply the momentum equation in the horizontal direction.

Find the velocities using the Energy equation.

Apply Newton’s Second Law to find the horizontal force.

Solution

Show code cell outputs

Conservation of mass:

\[\begin{split} Q_1 = Q_2 \\ v_1 A_1 = v_2 A_2 \\ v_1 y_1 b = v_2 y_2 b \\ v_2 = \frac{y_1 v_1}{y_2} \end{split}\]

Conservation of Energy:

\[\begin{split} z_1 + \frac{P_1}{\rho g} + \frac{v_1^2}{2g} = z_2 + \frac{P_2}{\rho g} + \frac{v_2^2}{2g} + h_f \\ \frac{P_1}{\rho g} = y_1, \quad \frac{P_2}{\rho g} = y_2 \\ z_1 = z_2 \end{split}\]
\[\begin{split} \frac{v_1^2}{2g} = y_2 + \frac{v_2^2}{2g} + h_f - y_1 \\ \frac{v_1^2}{2g} = y_2 + \frac{y_1^2 v_1^2}{2g y_2^2} + h_f - y_1 \end{split}\]

Once the velocities at both sections are determined, the conservation of momentum can then be applied:

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x = \rho Q (v_2 - v_1) \\ F_1 - F_2 -R_x = \rho Q (v_2 - v_1) \end{split}\]

The centroid of the spillway is:

\[ \rho g \frac{y_1}{2} A_1 - \rho g \frac{y_2}{2} A_2 - F = \rho Q (v_2 - v_1) \]

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} b &= 100 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] y_{1} &= 20 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] y_{2} &= 1.500 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] h_{f} &= 1.200 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] v_{1} &= \left( \frac{ \left( y_{1} - y_{2} - h_{f} \right) \cdot 2 \cdot g }{ \left( \frac{ y_{1} }{ y_{2} } \right) ^{ 2 } - 1 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= \left( \frac{ \left( 20 - 1.500 - 1.200 \right) \cdot 2 \cdot 9.810 }{ \left( \frac{ 20 }{ 1.500 } \right) ^{ 2 } - 1 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= 1.386 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] v_{2} &= y_{1} \cdot \frac{ v_{1} }{ y_{2} } \\&= 20 \cdot \frac{ 1.386 }{ 1.500 } \\&= 18.476 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= v_{1} \cdot y_{1} \cdot b \\&= 1.386 \cdot 20 \cdot 100 \\&= 2771.333 \; \;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{1} &= b \cdot y_{1} \\&= 100 \cdot 20 \\&= 2000 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{2} &= b \cdot y_{2} \\&= 100 \cdot 1.500 \\&= 150.000 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot \frac{ y_{1} }{ 2 } \cdot A_{1} - \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot \frac{ y_{2} }{ 2 } \cdot A_{2} - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( v_{2} - v_{1} \right) \\&= 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot \frac{ 20 }{ 2 } \cdot 2000 - 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot \frac{ 1.500 }{ 2 } \cdot 150.000 - 1000 \cdot 2771.333 \cdot \left( 18.476 - 1.386 \right) \\&= 147734607.558 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{xMN} &= 147.735 \; \;\textrm{(MN)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

\[\begin{split} z_1+\frac {P_1}{\rho g}+\frac{v_1^2}{2g}=z_2+\frac {P_2}{\rho g}+\frac{v_2^2}{2g}\\ z_1= 0, z_2=0\\ \frac {P_1}{\rho g}+\frac{v_1^2}{2g}=\frac {P_2}{\rho g}+\frac{v_2^2}{2g}\\ P_2=(\frac {P_1}{\rho g}+\frac{v_1^2}{2g}-\frac{v_2^2}{2g}) \rho g \end{split}\]
